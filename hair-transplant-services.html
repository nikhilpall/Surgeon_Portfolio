What is Hair Transplant Surgery?

Hair transplant surgery is a procedure performed to treat hair loss or baldness. It involves moving hair follicles from one part of the body, typically the back or sides of the scalp (called the donor area), to the areas where hair is thinning or has completely stopped growing (known as the recipient area).

Hair Transplant Techniques :

  • Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT): Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT), also known as strip harvesting, is a surgical technique used in hair transplantation. It involves removing a strip of scalp tissue from the donor area, typically the back or sides of the scalp, and then dissecting it into individual follicular units for transplantation.
  • Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE): Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) is a surgical technique used in hair transplantation. It is one of the primary methods for harvesting individual hair follicles from the donor area, typically the back or sides of the scalp, for transplantation to areas of hair loss or thinning.

The most typical query from patients

The most frequent queries from patients to Dr. Vivek Supaha are included below, along with their answers.

Is hair transplant a painful procedure?

Hair transplant procedures are generally performed under local anesthesia, which means that the area being treated is numbed, and the patient should not feel any pain during the surgery. However, it is normal to experience some discomfort and mild soreness in the days following the procedure.

How long does it take to recover from a hair transplant?

The recovery time after a hair transplant varies from person to person, but most patients can expect to return to their normal activities within a week or two. Initially, there may be some redness, swelling, and scabbing in the transplanted area, but these typically subside within a few days.

Will the transplanted hair look natural?

Yes, the goal of a hair transplant is to create a natural-looking result. Skilled surgeons take into consideration factors like hairline design, hair density, and the direction of hair growth to achieve a natural appearance.

How long does it take to see the final results of a hair transplant?

Hair transplant results are not immediate, and it takes time for the transplanted hair follicles to establish themselves and start growing new hair. In general, you can expect to see some initial growth within a few months after the procedure. However, it usually takes around 6 to 12 months for the full results to become noticeable.

How long does a hair transplant procedure usually take?

The duration of a hair transplant procedure can vary depending on the extent of the transplant and the specific technique used. Generally, a typical hair transplant session can take anywhere from 4 to 8 hours. However, for more extensive cases or larger transplant areas, multiple sessions may be required, spaced several months apart.

Are there any long-term complications or side effects of a hair transplant?

Hair transplant surgeries are generally safe, and long-term complications are rare. However, like any surgical procedure, there can be potential risks and side effects. These can include infection, bleeding, temporary shock loss (where transplanted hair sheds before regrowth), numbness or discomfort in the donor or recipient area, and the possibility of an unnatural appearance if the procedure is not performed by a skilled surgeon.
